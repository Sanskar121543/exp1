About Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, affectionately known as the "Missile Man of India," was an illustrious scientist and the 11th President of India. Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, he was instrumental in India's space and missile development programs.

Dr. Kalam's contributions to the development of ballistic missile technology and the Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III) were pivotal. He also played a key role in India's Pokhran-II nuclear tests. As President from 2002 to 2007, he inspired the nation with his vision for a developed India and his dedication to empowering youth.

Among his numerous accolades are the Bharat Ratna, Padma Bhushan, and Padma Vibhushan. His legacy lives on through his teachings, writings, and vision for the future.
